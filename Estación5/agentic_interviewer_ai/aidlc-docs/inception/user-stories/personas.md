## Persona 1: The Candidate

**Name**: María Reyes
**Age**: 24
**Location**: Bogotá, Colombia
**Role**: Job seeker — applying for a customer service agent role at a BPO

> *"I just want a fair chance to show I'm good at this. If the process takes more than a week, I assume I didn't get it and I move on."*

### Context
María scrolls job boards on her phone between shifts at her current retail job. She applies to multiple positions simultaneously. She's familiar with WhatsApp and Telegram for daily communication but has never been interviewed by an AI. She has limited time — the screening needs to fit around her schedule, including evenings and weekends.

### Goals
- Complete the job application quickly without taking time off work
- Feel heard and respected throughout the process
- Know what happens next after the screening ends
- Get feedback or at least confirmation that her application was received

### Pains
- Long, complex application processes make her give up mid-way
- Not knowing where she stands in the process causes anxiety
- Cold, robotic interactions feel dismissive and impersonal
- Fear of being judged by an AI she doesn't trust

### How María uses EntreVista AI
- Clicks Telegram link from a job posting
- Reads the AI disclosure and consents
- Completes the screening in 15-25 minutes from her phone
- Receives confirmation of next steps
- Optionally rates her experience (NPS)

### Success Metrics for this persona
- Candidate NPS >= 4.0/5.0
- Screening completion rate >= 80%
- Screening duration: 15-25 minutes
- Re-engagement rate after drop-off: >= 20%

### Key Needs
- Clear identification that the interviewer is AI — no deception
- Conversational, adaptive questions that feel personalized
- Ability to pause and resume without losing progress
- Transparent next steps at the end
- No time pressure or artificial deadlines during the conversation

## Persona 2: The Operational Recruiter

**Name**: Carlos Mejía
**Age**: 31
**Location**: Medellín, Colombia
**Role**: Talent Acquisition Coordinator at a BPO with 2,000 agents

> *"My job is to fill 30 seats this week. Every day a seat is empty is money lost for the operation. I need candidates that are ready, not CVs I have to read."*

### Context
Carlos manages a queue of 50-200 candidate applications every week across 5-10 open roles. He conducts 8-12 screening calls per day. He uses ATS tools but mostly lives in spreadsheets and WhatsApp groups. He's not technical but is pragmatic — if a tool makes his day faster, he'll adopt it. If it adds work, he'll ignore it.

### Goals
- Review only pre-screened candidates who meet minimum criteria
- See structured evidence per candidate, not just a CV
- Make an Approve/Reject decision in under 3 minutes per candidate
- Understand where candidates dropped off to improve the funnel
- Maintain control — the AI assists him, not replaces him

### Pains
- Spending 70% of his day on repetitive screening calls
- Candidates who ghost after he schedules an interview
- Inconsistent information from candidates depending on who asked
- Losing qualified candidates because the process takes too long

### How Carlos uses EntreVista AI
- Receives dashboard notification when candidates complete screening
- Opens review queue filtered by "Highly Recommended"
- Reviews executive summary + key citations for each candidate
- Clicks Approve or Reject with a reason
- Monitors campaign analytics to flag quality issues

### Success Metrics for this persona
- Review time per candidate: < 3 minutes
- Approval rate: >= 70%
- Human-AI disagreement rate: < 20% (decreasing over time)
- Escalation alert response time: < 4 hours

### Key Needs
- Review queue with clear sorting and filtering
- Executive summary that answers: "Is this person worth interviewing?"
- Transcript access for candidates he wants to scrutinize
- Easy Approve/Reject workflow with required rejection reason
- Campaign-level analytics showing funnel health

## Persona 3: The Talent Acquisition Director

**Name**: Valeria Torres
**Age**: 42
**Location**: Ciudad de México, Mexico
**Role**: VP of Talent Acquisition at a BPO with 5,000 employees across 3 countries

> *"I need to hire 1,200 people this year to maintain headcount. My team is burning out. The only way out is automation — but it has to be defensible legally and it has to work."*

### Context
Valeria leads a team of 25 recruiters. She reports to the CHRO and is under constant pressure from Operations to fill vacancies faster. She has budget authority for recruitment tools up to a certain ticket size. She's experienced with HR technology, skeptical of overpromises, and highly sensitive to legal and compliance risk. She was burned by a biometric video screening tool that triggered a discrimination complaint — she won't repeat that mistake.

### Goals
- Reduce time-to-hire from 44 days to under 5 days
- Scale screening capacity without proportionally increasing headcount
- Demonstrate consistent, bias-free evaluation to Legal and CHRO
- Have a complete audit trail for any candidate complaint
- Launch the tool in under 48 hours for new campaigns

### Pains
- Her team spends 80% of their time on first-round screening
- Seasonal peaks (end-of-year retail) require 500 hires in 30 days — impossible manually
- One discrimination complaint can halt the entire hiring process
- Vendor tools that promise "AI" but deliver rule-based chatbots

### How Valeria uses EntreVista AI
- Creates campaigns with custom rubrics for each role type
- Configures knowledge base with role-specific FAQs and policies
- Monitors campaign-level dashboard for volume, completion, and quality KPIs
- Reviews consistency reports before presenting to the CHRO
- Exports compliance reports for legal review

### Success Metrics for this persona
- Time-to-first-value: < 48 hours to first completed screening
- Campaign setup time: < 30 minutes
- Screening capacity: unlimited concurrent candidates
- Traceability: 100% of evaluations with textual citation
- Time-to-screen: < 1 hour from application to completed screening

### Key Needs
- Intuitive campaign creation with role templates
- Rubric editor with competency weights
- Knowledge base upload and management
- Campaign analytics with export capability
- Compliance-ready audit trail and data retention controls

## Persona 4: The Head of People / CHRO

**Name**: Andrés Vargas
**Age**: 38
**Location**: Bogotá, Colombia
**Role**: Head of People at a Series B tech startup with 120 employees (fintech)

> *"I hire for culture and skills. A bad hire at this stage of the company costs us 6 months and a lot of morale. Speed matters, but quality matters more."*

### Context
Andrés runs a lean People team of 3 (himself + 2 recruiters). They're hiring 60 engineers, PMs, and designers this year. He values candidate experience as an extension of employer brand — in the tech market, word spreads fast if your interview process is impersonal. He's comfortable with AI tools, uses Notion and Linear, and expects software to just work. He's the decision-maker for People tools with a moderate budget.

### Goals
- Evaluate technical and cultural competencies in the screening stage — not just keyword matching
- Ensure every candidate has a positive experience regardless of outcome
- Reduce the percentage of first-round screenings handled by his recruiters
- Have defensible, consistent evaluations in case of candidate disputes
- Scale from 1-5 hires/month to 10-15 hires/month without hiring more recruiters

### Pains
- Technical candidates have 3-5 competing offers — slow processes lose them
- His small team can't scale screening without burning out
- Every recruiter asks different questions — no consistency in evaluation
- "Culture fit" decisions feel subjective and legally risky

### How Andrés uses EntreVista AI
- Configures custom rubrics for tech roles (engineering, QA, design)
- Reviews executive summaries for mid-level and senior candidates
- Uses transcript evidence to validate AI recommendations
- Monitors candidate NPS to protect employer brand
- Accesses audit logs to respond to candidate inquiries

### Success Metrics for this persona
- Candidate NPS: >= 4.2/5.0 (higher bar than BPO segment)
- Quality-of-hire metric: screening-to-offer conversion >= 60%
- Human-AI alignment: r >= 0.75 per competency
- Recruiter time saved: 70% reduction in first-round screening hours

### Key Needs
- Custom rubrics for technical competencies
- Executive summaries that capture narrative evidence (not just scores)
- Candidate NPS tracking and alerts for poor experiences
- Role-based access so recruiters only see their assigned campaigns
- Data export for analysis and stakeholder reporting
